About Me

My name is Yuxuan Liu, from China and currently studying in Nohara Laboratory at Institute of Science Tokyo. I am a PhD candidate specializing in computational typography, focusing on stroke-based font representation and vector graphics processing.

Research Projects

Font Skeleton Generation

This project explores generating the skeletons from outlined glyphs by fine-tuning the LLMs (Large Language Models) to address the challenges posed by the absence of font skeletons in typeface design and automatic font generation.

  • Publication (Poster presentation on ACCV2024 in Hanoi, Vietnam): Yuxuan Liu, Yasuhisa Fujii, Xinru Zhu, and Kayoko Nohara. 2024. Fine-tuning Large Language Models for Automatic Font Skeleton Generation: Exploration and Analysis. In Proceedings of the Asian Conference on Computer Vision. 4156 - 4171.
  • Presentation (Oral presentation on PRMU2024 in Tokyo, Japan): Yuxuan Liu, Yasuhisa Fujii, Xinru Zhu, and Kayoko Nohara. 2024. Fine-tuning Large Language Models for Automatic Font Skeleton Generation: A First Attempt. IEICE technical report, vol. 124, no. 23, PRMU2024-2, pp. 7-12.
